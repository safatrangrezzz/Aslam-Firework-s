Fireworks Safety Guide

Fireworks create unforgettable moments, but safety should always be the first priority. Follow these professional safety guidelines to enjoy fireworks responsibly and protect yourself and others.

  • Always use fireworks outdoors in an open and safe area away from homes, vehicles, trees and electric wires.
  • Children should never handle fireworks alone — adult supervision is mandatory.
  • Maintain a minimum distance of 10 feet after lighting any firework.
  • Never relight, touch, or approach a firework that fails to ignite.
  • Keep a bucket of water, sand or a fire extinguisher nearby for emergencies.
  • Avoid loose or synthetic clothing. Cotton wear is the safest option.
  • Always read and follow the instructions on every firework product.
  • Do not use fireworks under the influence of alcohol or intoxicants.
  • Store fireworks in a cool, dry place — away from flames and heat.
  • Pour water on burnt fireworks before disposing of them safely.
